01 / TIMING

Target October 15–25.

Mid-to-late October is the sweet spot. Days in the upper 60s to upper 70s, nights drop to mid-40s, with monsoon season fully done and winter chill not yet arrived.

October in Sedona averages a high of 67.1°F and a low of 44.6°F, with only about 5 days of rain totaling roughly half an inch (weather-atlas data). The first half of the month still runs slightly warmer (occasional 80s) — the back half cools off enough for solid sleep in the rigs without overheating.

Get to camp Sunday through Tuesday. Spring and fall are Sedona's busy seasons — sites on FR 525 fill by Friday afternoon. Trailheads should be hit at sunrise.

02 / BASECAMP

Forest Road 525.

Free dispersed camping on Coconino National Forest land, 10 miles SW of Sedona, central to almost everything.

Loy Butte Rd / FR 525 has five designated dispersed camping areas: Surprise, Windmill, Cockscomb, Greasy Spoon, and Nolan. Nolan is the largest at ~12 acres — aim for it with the Promaster and F250. Graded dirt road, accessible for both rigs without 4WD. Coconino NF info →

No water, no toilets, no services. Showers in Cottonwood (20 min SW). Cell service is spotty — download Trailforks and onX Offroad for offline use.

    05 / E-DIRT BIKES

    Legal trails only. No singletrack.

    Important: Sur-Ron / Talaria-class e-dirt bikes are NOT classified as e-bikes in Coconino NF. They're treated as OHV / motorized vehicles.

    Per Thunder Mountain Bikes' official guidance: "eBikes are not permitted on ANY singletrack trails within the Coconino National Forest Red Rock District." High-power throttle machines fall even more strictly into the OHV bucket. They are not street legal without registration — so you'll need to trailer them to OHV trailheads.

      Full route list at Coconino NF — Sedona OHV Routes. 15 mph speed limit, helmets required.

        Q06 2026-05-24

        Quiet slot-canyon scrambles to add as alternatives to the icons?

        Two strong picks, both now on the map as hike pins:

        • Loy Canyon Trail — literally next to basecamp (~8 min drive). Red-walled canyon floor, dry streambed, with a real Class 3 hand-and-foot scramble if you push the full 5.9 mi to the Secret Mountain saddle. Most parties turn around at 2.5-3.5 mi, still worth the trip. The closest hike on the map.
        • Secret Canyon + HS Canyon — the slottiest "trail" hike in the Dry Creek drainage. Permanent stream, pools, boulder-filled narrow channels with short scrambles to bypass dryfalls. HS Canyon branches off as an even quieter side option. ~30-40 min drive — but FR 152's last 3.4 mi needs high-clearance 4WD.

        Skipped: Sterling Pass / Vultee Arch (forest grind, not slot character), Wilson Canyon (too flat), Munds Mountain (faint trail + needs HC vehicle to even get to it), Casner Mountain (~22-permit annual lottery, not realistically accessible), full-length Long Canyon (duplicates existing flat hikes).

        Honest gap: "True" technical slots in the area (Sycamore, Robbers Roost, Soldier Pass tech route) are canyoneering with rope and swim sections — out of scope for a hiking trip without that gear and training.

        Q05 2026-05-24

        Anything else worth adding that fits "active, outdoor, non-touristy, cook-our-own-food" criteria?

        Five candidates that match the brief, in rough priority order:

        • Rock climbing & bouldering. Sedona's red sandstone is world-class for trad — classic lines on Mace, The Cibola Mitten, Coyote Tower; quieter roadside bouldering near the Y. Non-touristy if you skip guided trips. Mountain Project / Mountaineering Project have full route lists. Could become its own pin category if anyone in the crew climbs.
        • Astrophotography from basecamp. FR 525 is genuinely dark-sky. New moon falls Oct 20, 2026 — the trip window hits it perfectly. No new pins needed — just a tip on basecamp.
        • FPV / drone flying spots (with no-go zones mapped). Paul and Winston already know the FAA rules; the local wrinkle is Coconino NF + the new Sedona Wilderness restrictions. Mapping where you CAN fly (Verde Valley BLM, Hwy 89A pullouts west of Sedona) would save time.
        • Page Springs / Cornville wine corridor. ~30 min drive (between Sedona and Cottonwood). Three walk-between tasting rooms (Page Springs Cellars, Oak Creek Vineyards, Javelina Leap). Buy-and-take-back-to-camp friendly. Low key, not bus-tour energy.
        • Slot scrambles beyond Boynton's Subway Cave. Secret Canyon and Casner Mountain area have quieter slot-style hikes if the icons are crowded.

        Things I'm not suggesting (they violate the brief): Pink Jeep tours, vortex tours, hot-air balloons, Slide Rock for actual swimming in Oct, and all the Ancestral Puebloan sites (Honanki, Palatki, Montezuma's Castle — interesting but they're history).

        Q04 2026-05-24

        What are bugs like in mid-to-late October?

        Verdict: very low. October is one of the best months for bug avoidance in Sedona — dry climate, cool nights, monsoon long over. Most pressure is from the standard desert critters (scorpions, snakes) rather than biting insects.

        • Mosquitoes / biting flies / no-see-ums: minimal. Very little standing water, low humidity, cool overnights.
        • Yellow jackets: still active early October, mostly gone by the 25th.
        • Scorpions: present year-round (Arizona bark scorpion is the medically significant one), but less active in cool weather. Shake out boots, don't leave shoes on the ground overnight, headlamp at night, glance before grabbing rocks.
        • Spiders: black widows present but rarely encountered.
        • Rattlesnakes: often denning up by mid-October, but a warm afternoon can bring them out. Watch foot and hand placement on slickrock.
        • Ticks: low risk in Sedona compared to most regions of the U.S.

        Net: bring DEET only as a precaution, not a daily ritual. Reflexive desert habits matter more than bug spray.

        Q03 2026-05-24

        Hot springs and swimming opportunities within reach?

        Four Oct-friendly options inside ~1.5 hr drive of basecamp:

        • Fossil Creek (Waterfall Trail): 70–72°F spring-fed travertine pools, 8 mi RT hike, ~90–100 min drive. Permit-free after Oct 1 — the dates work without reservations. Agent's top pick.
        • Verde Hot Springs: 98–104°F free natural soak with old resort ruins, clothing-optional. ~2 hr drive; last 6 mi is high-clearance dirt + a thigh-deep river ford. Hot water means it's perfect any season.
        • The Crack at Wet Beaver Creek (Bell Trail): deep red-rock slot pool with cliff jumping, ~45–55 min drive, 7 mi RT hike. Water runs low 60s in October — quick dip only, but the hike is great regardless.
        • Watson Lake (Prescott): granite-shore reservoir with kayak/SUP rentals through Oct 31, ~75–85 min drive. Paddle only — don't swim.

        Already on the map: Slide Rock State Park. By Oct 15 Oak Creek is ~55–65°F — photo stop and wading only, not real swimming.

        Skip: Cibecue Falls (3+ hr, swimming prohibited), Salt River tubing (season closed), Lake Powell / Havasupai (out of range).

        Q02 2026-05-24

        Any Blue-grade "drop in and ride down" shuttle trails?

        Honest answer: Sedona doesn't really have one. Every named shuttle line in town (Thunder Mountain Express, Highline) is Black/expert with cliff exposure. The closest Blue substitutes:

        • Llama → Slim Shady → Bell Rock Pathway (~6–7 mi, ~400–500 ft descent, all Blue, no exposure). Drop at Little Horse TH, pickup at Bell Rock Vista — both on paved Hwy 179, and the free Sedona Shuttle covers Little Horse Thu–Sun. Now on the map as "Shuttle Run: Llama → Slim Shady → Bell Rock".
        • Templeton → HT (~3.5 mi mostly descending, one Black pitch near the finish most strong intermediates walk). Same Little Horse drop, pickup at Back O' Beyond TH. Now on the map as "Shuttle Run: Templeton → HT".

        Expect short pedaly bits between the descending sections — neither is a sustained gravity run. Aerie/Cockscomb from basecamp is the closest "cruise without consequences" option that's already adjacent to camp, but it's not a true shuttle.
